# 📚 Paisabazaar Fraud Analysis Dashboard Using Power BI

## 📖 Project Overview
This project focuses on fraud detection and credit risk management using Power BI. The analysis explores customer risk across different dimensions such as occupation, age, credit score, repayment delays, and monthly trends. The goal is to identify high‑risk groups and provide insights that can help in proactive fraud prevention.

 ## Tool Used
 - Power BI

## 🔎 Key Insights
- **Risk by Occupation**: Engineers and scientists had the highest flagged counts, while accountants and entrepreneurs showed elevated risk due to financial exposure.
- **Risk by Age**: Young adults (18–25) were most vulnerable, middle‑aged groups (36–50) showed high risk due to financial commitments, and older customers (50+) had the lowest flagged counts.
- **Credit Risk Segmentation**:
  - Good score customers carried high debt but manageable EMIs.
  - Poor score customers had massive debt and repayment stress.
  - Standard score customers showed hidden risks with the highest EMI and delays.
- **Repayment Delays**: Standard score customers recorded the highest delays, even more than poor score customers.
- **Monthly Trends**: Risk peaked in June, showing seasonal or cyclical patterns, and stabilized in July–August.

  ## 💡 Suggestions
- Occupation‑specific monitoring for engineers, scientists, accountants, and entrepreneurs.
- Age‑based strategies such as financial literacy programs for young adults and debt restructuring for middle‑aged customers.
- Tiered credit score policies: stricter limits for poor scores, leverage monitoring for good scores, and special focus on standard scores.
- Early warning systems for repayment delays with reminders or restructuring offers.
- Seasonal monitoring during mid‑year peaks to strengthen fraud detection.

## 📝 Conclusion
The Paisabazaar Fraud Analysis shows that risk is concentrated among younger customers, certain occupations, and those with poor or inconsistent repayment behavior. Seasonal peaks highlight the importance of timely monitoring and targeted interventions. This dashboard provides a strong foundation for fraud risk management and proactive decision‑making.
